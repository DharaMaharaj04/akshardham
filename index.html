🌉 Chenab Bridge: Where Steel Meets the Sky

A triumph of engineering in the Himalayas

High in the Himalayas, where the Chenab River carves through gorges, India dared to dream big. This bridge is more than metal and bolts — it’s a tale of vision, perseverance, and an engineering marvel that connects hearts and valleys.

🏗️ From Dream to Reality

  • Part of USBRL project to connect Kashmir
  • Located in Reasi district, Jammu & Kashmir
  • Bridge height: 359m — higher than Eiffel Tower
  • Length: 1,315m | Arch span: 467m

🏆 World Record Bridge

  • Highest railway bridge on Earth
  • Withstands winds up to 266 km/h
  • Built using 28,000 tonnes of steel
  • Completed in 2022, trial run in Aug 2023

📸 Gallery & Videos

🔩 Engineering Marvels

  • Blast-proof steel used in key zones
  • Wind tunnel tested in Denmark & Germany
  • Longest cable crane system: 915m span
  • Constructed without ground support
  • Temperature extremes: -20°C to 45°C

👷 Human Stories & Safety First

  • 1,300 workers & 300 engineers braved nature
  • Automatic sensors for wind, temperature, seismic activity
  • Blast-resistant design by DRDO input
  • Seismic Zone IV ready

🌟 Symbolism & Significance

The Chenab Bridge is a symbol of unity, strategic strength, and national pride. It integrates the Kashmir Valley, supports troop movement, and attracts tourism.

  • Time capsule buried during construction
  • Visible from satellite imagery
  • Future plans include museum, tours, and photo points
